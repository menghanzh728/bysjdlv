英国文化教育协会（ **British Council**）与 **LUX** 携手呈现的“此时彼刻——英国朋克后的电影与录像”。该影展收集了大量后朋克时代（ **1979 - 1985**）艺术家的电影和视频，该项目与英国电影协会（ **BFI**）国家档案馆合作开发，共包括七个放映单元。

**20** 世纪 **80** 年代初，俱乐部会员、艺术专业学生、新浪漫主义和后朋克成员们，不约而同地使用廉价的家用技术试图寻找新的表达方式并颠覆主流媒体。随之而来的，是独立录像带得以发布，超 **8** 电影被作为一种廉价的但明显抒情而直接的新媒体被人们接受了。朋克的 **DIY** 方式得到了有力的重生。

本次影展聚焦 **20** 世纪 **80** 年代初，那些探索媒体形象和身份认同之间模糊界限的作品，这种探索创造出的是自我与世界之间的全新对话。后朋克时代是一个充满了不确定的、政治上有争议的时代；是一个类似于今天互联网时代的时间段；是一个个放映单。在这样的时代中，艺术家们不禁思考：图像和技术意味着什么？它们最充分的意义又是什么？

!

!

_格雷森·佩里 **Grayson Perry**，现在是颇有名头的特纳奖获得者和顶尖艺术院校的领导，那时还是一个挣扎的准艺术家，在 **1984** 年向艺术委员会申请的个人陈述中说：“对于我来说，我拍摄电影开始于一架一磅三十便士的相机，十年之前的一卷旧胶卷。从那时起，我就把自己看作是一个流行音乐世界中的某种土鳖电影制片人。”他继续说道：“尽管是在十八个月的被救济时日，我也竭力扮演了离经叛道的艺术家角色”（ **Perry 1984**）。—— 威廉·弗勒_

_相比之下，艾萨克·朱利安 **Isaac Julien** 的电影 **Territories**（ **1984**）则研究了当时的文化景观。技术及其各种平台，将它们揭露为持有和延续性的，不同的特定等级仪式，这反过来又引发了对诺丁山狂欢节等重要黑人文化事件的遮蔽，导致进一步的种族陈规定型，边缘化和控制。这部电影编辑和交叉剪辑从 **Super 8**，标准 **8mm**， **16mm** 和电视报道而来的所有内容，到最后以不同的序列和抗议音乐巧妙地以 **DJ** 的方式来回混合，最终抵制具体的表现和解决方案。—— 威廉·弗勒_

**This is Now: Film and Video After Punk, a major new touring project that looks at artists’ film and video from the post-punk era (1979–85) is coming to China. The project is co-organised by the British Council and LUX, comprises seven screening programmes and is developed in partnership with the BFI National Archive.**

**In the early 1980s, clubbers, art students, New Romantics and members of the post-punk scene used inexpensive, domestic technology to find new modes of expression and subvert the mainstream media. Independent VHS tapes were released, stridently bypassing censorship, and Super 8 film was embraced as a cheap yet distinctly lyrical and direct new medium. The DIY approach of punk was powerfully reborn.**

**These programmes focus on work from the early 1980s that explore the blurred lines between media images and identity, creating new dialogues between the self and the world. It was an uncertain, politically contentious time; a time in which – very much like today with the internet – technology appeared to ease life, yet also created gaps between people. Artists considered what images and technology could mean and be in their fullest sense.**

!

**_Grayson Perry, now a celebrity Turner Prize winner, then a struggling would-be artist, said in a personal statement attached to a 1984 Arts Council application: ‘filmmaking for me began with a camera for one pound thirty pence and a ten-year-old reel of film. Since then I have regarded myself as some kind of skiffle filmmaker in a world of pop video.’  He continued: ‘despite being on the dole for eighteen months I have played out my role of eccentric artist with much enthusiasm’ (Perry 1984)._** _\-\- William Fowler_

**_Isaac Julien’s film Territories (1984), in contrast, examined the cultural landscape of the time.  Technology and its various platforms are exposed as holding and perpetuating different specific hierarchical agendas, which in turn problematise the coverage of important black cultural events such as the Notting Hill Carnival, leading to further racial stereotyping, marginalisation and control. The film weaves and cross-cuts everything from Super 8, standard 8mm, 16mm and television reportage, and by the end different sequences and protest music are deftly mixed back and forth in the manner of a DJ, ultimately resisting concrete representation and resolution._** _\-\- William Fowler_
