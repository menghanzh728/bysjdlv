现在，我们要如何去生活，如何去活出自己的电影？对于浸淫在网络中我们自身的存在来说，技术意味着什么？此时，这个问题正是彼刻的问题：超8摄影机在朋克后时代的问题。此时彼刻，技术上的“解放”，技术上更加自由下放的电影与录像技术意味着什么？作为群众、观众、演员、摄影师、导演、编剧，我们要如何重新掌控沦陷在各种界面中的身体？或许，通过一次次重掌你自己手中的摄制机器，这个问题的答案只能只能在此时，譬如，在快手后的时代，由热爱电影和拍摄的你来操作给出。

此次放映和谈话活动，我们有幸邀请到二位英国女性导演和艺术家来放映相关作品和展开讨论；跨越英国朋克后，并直到到现在中国的现场，她们将同时身兼彼刻的荣耀和此时的好奇。我们也有幸邀请到项目相关机构的专家、艺术家、纪录片导演和艺术专业的师生等共同参与交流，欢迎大家参与。

“重摄身体的界面”《此时彼刻——英国朋克后的电影与录像》系列活动之一。《此时彼刻》是即将在南京艺术学院美术馆AMNUA国际计划III项目中呈现的展览项目之一，围绕这一展览，将在项目期间和展览前后开展一系列的公共活动。

**朱迪思·戈达德**

朱迪思·戈达德（Judith Goddard）的作品跨越了移动影像发展的三十多年。从20世纪80年代开始，她以其突破性的沉浸式大型视觉装置艺术而广为所知，作品已被广泛地展示在包括泰特利物浦美术馆、泰特不列颠美术馆、泰特现代美术馆、牛津艺术博物馆、剑桥茶壶院美术馆，以及葡萄牙、美国、日本、法国、印度和中国等国家。她惯用不同的媒介，包括移动和静止的影像、绘画、雕塑和印刷等。视觉和时间，无论是从个人的还是历史的角度来看，都是戈达德作品的基本组成部分，她娴熟地使用观察、选择和构建过程来传递美感。戈达德毕业于雷丁大学和皇家艺术学院；在伦敦大学学院斯莱德美术学院兼职并在伦敦生活和工作。其作品由苏格兰倒带英国项目和英格兰英国电影协会所收藏，早期的单幕作品由Lux发行。

**万达·卡特**

万达·卡特（Vanda Carter）于1958年出生在伦敦，与她的伴侣和两个孩子生活在伦敦南部的布里克斯顿。万达（Vanda）在牛津大学布雷齐诺斯学院修习过英语，于1981年毕业，随后在克罗伊登艺术设计学院学习电影制作（1982-1983年）。1983—1986年间，她在伦敦电影制作人协会经营前卫电影发行库，并在南泰晤士河继续教育学院和斯特兰德电影工作室任教，负责讲授电影制作和动画。万达（Vanda）是一位多媒体艺术家，不同时期在进行着电影、视频、插图、诗歌、雕塑和小说创作等各个领域的工作。她的作品里经常将各种媒体搭配在一起。在她的作品中，她经常地将文本和图像组合或关联在一起。目前她在从事建筑发展方面的兼职工作，以及进行金属雕塑、书籍插图和冷水游泳运动。

How should we live our own lives and our own films? What does technology mean in our internet-saturated existences? Right now, these questions are related to something from another time: Super 8cameras in the post-punk era. Right now, what does technical “liberation” mean? What do freer film and recording technologies for the masses mean? As people, audiences, performers, photographers, directors, and screenwriters, how should we re-take control of bodies that are drowning in interfaces? Perhaps by re-taking control of the camera in your hand, this question can be answered in the present moment. In an era after the advent of Kuaishou, the film and filmmaking fan in you can provide an answer.

We are honored to have two British women directors and artists presenting their work and hosting discussions. Spanning British punk and the present site in China, they will share the glory of their past experiences and their curiosity about the present. We have invited experts, artists, documentary directors, and art students and teachers from a range of institutions to participate in the discussion, and of course, we also welcome the participation of the general public.

“Re-Filming the Interface with the Body” is one of the events associated with “This is Now: Film and Video After Punk.” “This is Now” is one of the exhibitions in the AMNUA International Project III. The museum will hold a series of public events related to the exhibition while it is on view.

**Judith Goddard**

Judith Goddard’s work spans over three decades of the moving image.

Known for her ground breaking immersive large-scale video installations dating from the1980’s, her work has been shown widely, including Tate’s Liverpool, Britain and Modern, the Museum of Art Oxford, Kettles Yard Cambridge, with installations shown internationally in Portugal, the U.S. Japan, France, India and China.

Her practice incorporates a diverse range of media, including the moving and still image, drawing, sculpture, and print. Vision and time, both from a personal and or historic perspective are fundamental constituents of Goddard’s work with the aesthetic informed by a highly adept process of observation, selection and construction.

Goddard studied at the University of Reading, and the Royal College of Art; she teaches part-time at the Slade School of Fine Art, University College London, and lives and works in London.

The work is archived by Rewind a UK project based in Scotland, and by the British Film Institute in England, early single screen works are distributed by the Lux.

**Vanda Carter**

Vanda Carter was born in London in 1958. She lives in Brixton, South London with her partner and their two children.

Vanda studied English at Brasenose College, Oxford, graduating in 1981. She then studied film making at Croydon College of Art and Design (1982-3). She ran the avant-garde film distribution library at the London Film Maker's Co-operative (1983-86) and has taught film production and animation at South Thames Further Education College and the Strand Film Workshop.

Vanda is a multi-media artist, working at various times in film, video, illustration, poetry, sculpture and fiction writing, frequently mixing media together. Text and image are very often combined or inter-related in her work. She currently works part-time in building development and divides the rest of her time between metal sculpture, book illustration and cold water swimming.

**关于《此时彼刻——英国朋克后的电影与录像》：**

**此时彼刻**

——英国朋克后的电影与录像

英国文化教育协会（British Council）与LUX携手呈现的“此时彼刻——英国朋克后的电影与录像”。该影展收集了大量后朋克时代（1979- 1985）艺术家的电影和视频，该项目与英国电影协会（BFI）国家档案馆合作开发，共包括七个放映单元。

20世纪80年代初，俱乐部会员、艺术专业学生、新浪漫主义和后朋克成员们，不约而同地使用廉价的国内技术试图寻找新的表达方式并颠覆主流媒体。随之而来的，是独立录像带得以发布，超8电影被作为一种廉价的但明显抒情而直接的新媒体被人们接受了。朋克的DIY方式得到了有力的重生。

本次影展聚焦20世纪80年代初，那些探索媒体形象和身份认同之间模糊界限的作品，这种探索创造出的是自我与世界之间的全新对话。后朋克时代是一个充满了不确定的、政治上有争议的时代；是一个类似于今天互联网时代的时间段天互联网时代满了不确定的、政治上有争议的时代；是一个个放映单。在这样的时代中，艺术家们不禁思考：图像和技术意味着什么？它们最充分的意义又是什么？

**Abut _THIS IS NOW_ \- Film and Video After Punk：**

THIS  IS  NOW

Film and Video After Punk

This is Now: Film and Video After Punk, a major new touring project that looks at artists’ film and video from the post-punk era (1979–85) is coming to China. The project is co-organised by the British Council and LUX, comprises seven screening programmes and is developed in partnership with the BFI National Archive.

In the early 1980s, clubbers, art students, New Romantics and members of the post-punk scene used inexpensive, domestic technology to find new modes of expression and subvert the mainstream media. Independent VHS tapes were released, stridently bypassing censorship, and Super 8 film was embraced as a cheap yet distinctly lyrical and direct new medium. The DIY approach of punk was powerfully reborn.

These programmes focus on work from the early 1980s that explore the blurred lines between media images and identity, creating new dialogues between the self and the world. It was an uncertain, politically contentious time; a time in which – very much like today with the internet – technology appeared to ease life, yet also created gaps between people. Artists considered what images and technology could mean and be in their fullest sense.
