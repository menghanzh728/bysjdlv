**金陵冬季学院**

真实总那么麻烦：一些事情发生了、存在了、消失了，历史随之在层叠记载的字里行间变得可疑，例如上海美专、南京总统府和金陵冬季学院。

金陵冬季学院，镜像中的学院，如烟消逝一个世纪的学院，在秘密知情者的小圈子里口耳相传的学院，随时准备丢弃和重建的学院。海市蜃楼。石头城的结界。

在一个类似谜题般的故事设计中，“虚构”，在我的头脑中运作着，脑细胞展开乱针刺绣的地图。引用、考古、档案、模拟、比喻、造假……修辞术雪崩般涌来。比邻着虚构的真实如此不安：谁在窗口赏雪？

那是一个永远无法完成的庞杂计划，金陵冬季学院的同仁们却依然抱有天真的雄心壮志，有点像某种文革期间发展起来的前卫艺术小团体，从1949年到今天，金陵冬季学院组织严密、暗中发生，从未被人觉察。

此次展览首要任务，就是用“展览它”这一动作把已经发生了的变成虚构的。当修辞的雪崩涌向窗口，发出震耳欲聋的声响时，我一拉窗帘，说：“停！”

展览。《陌生女人来信》里的信。通过一片雪花，来怀疑整个雪景。

上个月，刘林微信发给我一个命题“学院建筑”。我马上给他推送了一个名片，说“这事儿你得找他”。

“秦槐，1966年毕业于金陵冬季学院，现在工作生活在南京。”

——于瀛

_于瀛，1987年生于山东，“阳台”发起人和成员之一，先后毕业于清华大学（学士）和中央美院（硕士）。他以绘画、拼贴和影像为主的创作实践，来源于他对社会和政治现实的观察和感知。他曾获得今日美术馆十年回顾特别奖（2015）、关注未来英才计划入围（2011）、中国新锐绘画奖入围（2011）等奖项。作品曾在休斯顿当代艺术博物馆、柏林世界文化宫，巴黎GaîtéLyrique艺术中心、都灵卫城博物馆、西班牙瓦伦西亚大学美术馆、上海当代艺术博物馆、民生现代美术馆（上海、北京）、北京今日美术馆等美术馆展出或放映，公共收藏包括今日美术馆、清华大学美术馆、中央美术学院美术馆和香港当代艺术馆等。_

**策展人语**

南艺美术馆的“0空间”给我的感受就像它的名字一样，像是漂浮在学院里的临时空间。因为“0空间”而起的“零方案”，也可以说是无中生有，间歇性的闪现又湮灭在学院美术馆的身体里，但不管怎么说，它仍然是学院建筑的一部分。这种互相依存而又摧毁的关系是我一直感兴趣的，“零方案”长达三年半的实践实际上也伴随着这一缠斗、周旋与协商，直到眼下还是如此。当我有机会在“0空间”中策划一个项目时，我就希望说说“她自己”或与这相关的事儿。

“金陵冬季学院”（1949-）就是隐匿在时空边缘的一段往事，即使这边缘也许一点都不重要。那些怀有激进的美学与政治主张的艺术家从来没有被人们提起过，他们就像历史里默默无闻的大多数，钻在空虚里寻找价值或失败。艺术家于瀛把这段南艺校史里不为人知的“反学院的学院史”调取出来，然后用他擅长的“图像证虚/史”的方式去勾画这所地下学院中的人与事。在这些人与事中当然充斥着各种“反”的情绪，同时你又将见到作为学院建筑—作为一种坚固的又有弹性的，逼仄但又能设法通过的可以体验的空间，一套控制与教育体系的变身。最终的结果是，学院与反学院互相作为对方的幽灵，飘荡在这小小的展厅中。

“金陵冬季学院”差不多就是“0空间”或者所有那些若即若离的个人、组织和团体在历史中的镜像，但谁是谁的虚像呢？

—— 刘林

_刘林，策展人，撰稿人，生活和工作在南京，任四方当代美术馆展览总监。他是四方当代美术馆长期在地项目“地形学”的主要发起人之一。_

Nanking Winter College

Realityis a hassle all the time. Some things happened, appeared, and thendisappeared, with history buried in the lines of recordsbecoming untrustable, like Shanghai College of Fine Arts, the PresidentialPalace and Nanking Winter College. Nanking Winter College, is a collegewhich appeared in the mirror image, and disappeared like a wisp of smokeone century ago; it is a college could only be heard among insiders, andwould be discarded and rebuilt again anytime. Mirage. Baisema (boundaries)of the Stone City.

In apuzzle-like storyline design, “fictionalization” is working in my brainwhile cells opened a messily-embroidered map. Quotation, archaeology,archiving metaphor, metonymy, falsification. The avalanche of rhetoricstormed in. Reality, right next to virtuality, feels so disturbed, “Who isenjoying this snow by the window?”

Thatis a jumbled plan that will never end, however, all the fellowsfrom Nanking Winter College still held their naive ambition,reminding people of those avant-garde artist groups in CulturalRevolution. Since 1949 until now, this school was secretly organized andnever noticed.

Theprime task of this exhibition is to turn something which hasalready happened into something fictionalized by “exhibiting” it. Whenfloods of rhetoric were surging to the window and going to deafen my ears,I shut the curtain open and shouted, “Stop!”

Anexhibition. The letter from Letter from an Unknown Woman.To suspect thewhole snow scene, through a piece of snowflake.

LiuLin sent me a proposition named College Architecture on Wechat last month.And I immediately shared a contact card to him, saying “You need to getthis guy for this business.”

“Qinhuai,who graduated from Nanking Winter College in 1966, is now working andliving in Nanjing.”

\- YuYing

Curator’s statement

TheZero Program’s Space in Art Museum of Nanjing University of the Arts makes mehave a sense of feeling like the meaning of its name that it is like atemporary space which floats in the college. To some degree, the Zero Programwhich originates from the Zero Space is created out of nothing. It appearsintermittently and annihilates in the space of Art Museum of Nanjing Universityof the Arts. At all events, it is also a part of the college’s  structure.I am always interested in the interdependent and destructive relationship. Infact, till today, the three and a half years’ practice of the Zero Program isaccompanied by dogfight, entanglement and negotiation constantly. When I have achance to plan a project in the Zero Space, I would like to discuss more about“herself” or something about it.

NankingWinter College (1949-) is the past of Nanjing University of the Arts. It hidesin the edge of time and space, though the edge is not important at all. Theartists who hold the proposition of radical aesthetics and politics never bementioned by people, they just like most unknown people in the history who dovestraight into the emptiness to look for value and failure. Yu Ying takesthe unknown “anti-college college history” from the history of NanjingUniversity of the Arts to delineate the people and things in the college. Hetakes advantages of the way what he is good at to use images to prove thehistory is true or false. All these people and things are filled with variousfeelings of “anti”. Meanwhile, as a college structure oras a solid and elastic, packed and passable space which can be managed toexperience, you will both find a set of transformation of control and educationsystem. The final result is that college and anti-college as each other’s ghostfloat in the small hall.

Insome ways, Nanking Winter College is the mirror of the Zero Space or all thoseindividuals, groups and organizations which is neither friendly nor aloof inthe history. However, it is difficult to distinguish which is the virtualimage.

－ Liu Lin

_Liu Lin，curator andcopywriter，lives and works in Nanjing. He is the exhibition directorof Sifang Art Museum. He is also one of the main founders of Sifang ArtMuseum's long-term locality project “topography”._
