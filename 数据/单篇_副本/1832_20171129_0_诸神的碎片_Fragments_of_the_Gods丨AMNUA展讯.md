“诸神的碎片”是【图像论】系列展览的第二个项目，旨在从破坏、拆解和增生的通道来探讨当代图像与其生成和生存环境之间可能形成的反思关系。在当代生活中，“碎片”并不意味着被动和致损的结果，而更多是令人惯以为常的起点、一种主动的拆解和生成。如果说崇尚破坏和消解的后现代社会是以粉碎经典叙事和图像神话为其本质性的行为，那么在当代图像环境中，被不停介入、拆解和复制增生的片段和它们的动作系统本身就形成了新的图像神学。当代图像的世界不再是“一神”统摄的天地，而成为了诸神的狂欢之所。除了碎片状的生态，还包括图、像之间反反复复的接入、断叙和输出，以及语言操作上的喃喃不休所形成的幽灵般的图像回音。

由此展览选择了将四个带有不同针对性的创作样本放在同一个空间中予以对话。贾淳的《安德鲁斯三角形系列》与《片段》从新中国体育发展史这一叙述层面切入，对意识形态框架下的图像系统予以反思；宋佳益的《众神的黄昏》系列则是将图像剧场的方式探索历史图像与当代精神症候的相遇；王戈的《绿色饥渴症》以空间中彼此呼应的关系再现了消费神话所塑造的都市图像集；郑乐的《72还是4只半蜜蜂?》用逻辑与现象互译的方式展示了抽象理念的现象性和图像材质的抽象性对图像本身的撕裂。“诸神的碎片”作为当代图像创作的一个采集样本，将分别从意识形态挤压下的形象叙事、历史图像的考古研究、以及大众媒体与图像志的角度，尝试拆解和重组当代图像神学的光谱。

**关于策展人：**

**Mezzanine**

Mezzanine是多位中国年轻艺术家于2011年在法国巴黎注册成立的艺术协会组织。该协会以展览、讲座、研讨会等形式，在2011年至2013年期间为在法的青年艺术家提供创作、展示和讨论交流的平台。Mezzanine协会成员目前分布在欧洲、北京和上海等多地，持续以个体或集体的方式展开在地的展览与行动项目。

“Fragments of the Gods” is the second project in the “On Images” exhibition series, which aims to explore the contemplative relationships that can be formed through destruction,dissolution, and regeneration between contemporary images and the environments of their production and existence. In contemporary life, “fragments” are not the result of passivity and loss; they are a customary starting point, an active mode of destruction and production. If the essential act of post-modern society, which advocates destruction and dissolution, is destroying classic narratives and visual myths, then in the contemporary image environment, fragments that are constantly facing intervention, destruction, replication, and proliferation, and their systems of action, create a new theology of images. The world of contemporary images is no longer governed by A God; it has become a place of revelry for many gods. In addition to fragmentary ecologies, this world also includes the repeated intervention,disruption, and production of diagrams and images, as well as the ghostly pictorial echoes created by the loquacious manipulation of language.

The four creative practices with different focal points chosen for this exhibition are placed in the same space to engage in dialogue. Jia Chun’s _The Andrews Triangle_ and _Fragments_ begin with the historical narrative of sports in New China, reflecting on a system of images within an ideological framework. Song Jiayi’s _Twilight of the Gods_ is an exploration of the meeting of historical images and contemporary spiritual maladies through a theater of images. Wang Ge’s _Craving Green_ represents the metropolitan image collection produced by the myths of consumption through their echoing relationships in space. Zheng Le’s _72 or 4.5 Bees?_ interprets logic and phenomena through one another to present how the phenomenal qualities of abstract ideas and the abstract qualities of image materials tear apart the images themselves.“Fragments of the Gods” is a sampling of contemporary image creation, which attempts to deconstruct and reconstruct the spectrum of contemporary image theology from the perspectives of formal narratives under the pressure of ideology, the archeological study of historical images, as well as mass media and iconography.

Mezzanineis an artist association founded by young Chinese artists in 2011 and registered in Paris, France. From 2011 to2013, the association provided young artists living in France with a platform for creating, presenting, and discussing art through exhibitions, lectures, and symposia. Mezzanine members are currently living in cities across Europe, as well as Beijing and Shanghai, developing local exhibitions and art actions in individual and collective formats.
