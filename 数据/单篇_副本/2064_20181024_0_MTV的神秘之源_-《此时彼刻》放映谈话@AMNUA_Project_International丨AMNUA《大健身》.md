**关于嘉宾**

**威廉·福勒**

**威廉·福勒（William Fowler）**，作家、电影历史学家兼音乐家，英国电影协会国家档案馆（BFI National Archive）“艺术家移动影像展映”（Artists’ Moving Image）策展人。在英国电影协会期间，其主持的展映和电影修复项目包括“杰夫·基恩电影集”（GAZWRX: the films of Jeff Keen），“怪异的异教徒朋克：德里克·贾曼”（Queer Pagan Punk: Derek Jarman，这是有史以来规模最大的贾曼回顾展），以及“此时彼刻——英国朋克后的电影与录像”（This Is Now: Film and Video After Punk，目前正通过国际艺术机构LUX举行国际巡回展映）。此外，威廉还参与了《边缘即是中心：大卫·拉德金与“彭达的沼泽”》（The Edge is Where the Centre is: David Rudkin and Penda’s Fen）部分章节的撰写并担任该书的联合编辑。他是英国电影协会实验影片月展（Experimenta）的策划人，并自2013年起担任伦敦电影节实验影片单元的共同策划人之一。

**关于《此时彼刻——英国朋克后的电影与录像》**

!

英国文化教育协会（British Council）与LUX携手呈现的“ **此时彼刻——英国朋克后的电影与录像**”。该展收集了大量后朋克时代（1979- 1985）艺术家的电影和视频，该项目与英国电影协会（BFI）国家档案馆合作开发，共包括七个放映单元。

20世纪80年代初，俱乐部会员、艺术专业学生、新浪漫主义和后朋克成员们，不约而同地使用廉价的家用技术试图寻找新的表达方式并颠覆主流媒体。随之而来的，是独立录像带得以发布，超8电影被作为一种廉价的但明显抒情而直接的新媒体被人们接受了。朋克的DIY方式得到了有力的重生。

本次展览聚焦20世纪80年代初，那些探索媒体形象和身份认同之间模糊界限的作品，这种探索创造出的是自我与世界之间的全新对话。后朋克时代是一个充满了不确定的、政治上有争议的时代；是一个类似于今天互联网时代的时间段天互联网时代满了不确定的、政治上有争议的时代；是一个个放映单。在这样的时代中，艺术家们不禁思考：图像和技术意味着什么？它们最充分的意义又是什么？

****About the guest****

!

**William Fowler**

William Fowler is a writer, film historian and musician.  He is Curator of Artists’ Moving Image, BFI National Archive and his seasons and restoration projects at the BFI have included GAZWRX: the films of Jeff Keen, Queer Pagan Punk: Derek Jarman (the largest ever Jarman retrospective) and This Is Now: Film and Video After Punk, currently touring internationally through LUX.  He contributed chapters to and co-edited ‘The Edge is Where the Centre: David Rudkin and Penda’s Fen’. He programmes the monthly BFI strand Experimenta and has since 2013 been the co-programmer of Experimentain the London Film Festival.  He regularly gives talks and presents films.

****Abut _THIS IS NOW \- Film and Video After Punk_****

**This is Now: Film and Video After Punk**, a major new touring project that looks at artists’ film and video from the post-punk era (1979–85) is coming to China. The project is co-organised by the British Council and LUX, comprises seven screening programmes and is developed in partnership with the BFI National Archive.

In the early 1980s, clubbers, art students, New Romantics and members of the post-punk scene used inexpensive, domestic technology to find new modes of expression and subvert the mainstream media. Independent VHS tapes were released, stridently bypassing censorship, and Super 8 film was embraced as a cheap yet distinctly lyrical and direct new medium. The DIY approach of punk was powerfully reborn.

These programmes focus on work from the early 1980s that explore the blurred lines between media images and identity, creating new dialogues between the self and the world. It was an uncertain, politically contentious time; a time in which – very much like today with the internet – technology appeared to ease life, yet also created gaps between people. Artists considered what images and technology could mean and be in their fullest sense.
