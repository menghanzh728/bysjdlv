**一个画家的三十年**

文/李小山

很长时间里，沈勤的名字在人们的印象里并不清晰。他是画家，他画水墨画，1980年代就登台亮相，获得好评，然而在之后的岁月中，他若隐若现，用他自己的话说，几乎在石家庄隐居了三十年。沈勤是南京的画家，在石家庄“隐居”，似乎有些奇怪。奇怪的事发生在一个画家身上其实是很正常的，要是这个画家足够厉害，就更正常了。沈勤在水墨画领域里足够厉害，在我的视野力，能够与他匹敌的人屈指可数。我知道，这样的说法是有些可疑的，画画不是打擂台，谁赢谁输不靠勇力，所以匹不匹敌只是一种比喻。在某种程度上，奠定画家的地位需要时间，这个时间不是三年五年，很多时候三十年五十年都看不出来。我常常用筛子来形容——在筛子晃动下，泥土和小石子先掉下去，接着掉下的是中石头，最后只剩大石块留下来了。谁经得起时间这个筛子，谁就有地位，就是人们心目中的大画家。

!

水墨画以前叫中国画，近几十年来，由于中国画这一提法包涵内容太广，歧义太多，为方便起见，缩小范围，用材料命名，与油画、版画等画种一样，就容易解释和言说了。水墨画的现实处境显得尴尬，作为本土的传统艺术，我们热爱她，想着如何呵护她。但是，这个“我们”不包括许多头脑简单的人，更不包括利欲熏心、浑水摸鱼的人。因为水墨画看起来门槛不高，与大多数同胞又有着天然的亲和力，给后者提供了发挥的机会和空间。他们像广场舞大妈一样人多势众，也与广场舞大妈一样跳着最简单的舞步，却引来阵阵喝彩和掌声。在各种各类展览里，各种各类的交易中，最显眼的身影往往是水平低下的家伙。我曾和一位著名的画院院长开玩笑：你们那里不少画家顶着一级画师二级画师的名头，一天到晚画低层次重复的东西，甚至有的只是地摊货水平，不嫌烦么？我得赶紧补充一句，我没有贬低地摊画的意思，人家画画谋生，没什么好丢脸的。水墨画门槛低，水墨画有群众基础，但不能因此把她硬生生地弄成烂东西。尤其是那些专门从事这项工作的人，吃着皇粮，盆满钵满，总该有点尊严的意思，拿出一点真材实料吧？唐代以前不说，宋、元、明、清，那可是大师辈出；即使在民国，也留下了不少优秀的遗产。我们时代的画家本该奋起直追，为何反倒令人失望了呢？

!

沈勤言语不多，却往往能够画龙点睛，三言两语，直指问题的实质。我们聊得来，原因在于我们都直来直去，反感绕弯弯；都有精神的洁癖，容忍不了不干不净的东西。譬如他说了，恶俗的时代需要恶俗的画家。没错，事实的确如此。时代怎么会恶俗了呢？这件事说来话长：体制、市场、传统、人心等等，等等，各种因素交叉起来，你中有我，我中有你，不过，复杂归复杂，总有一些根本的因素在起作用。在我看来，标准的扭曲便是其中之一。本来，美丑优劣的标准摆那儿，人若有自知之明，对号入座就行了，问题是倒过来了，像莎士比亚的描述，黑的变成了白的，香的变成了臭的，美的变成了丑的。标准一变，就什么都变了。所以，一个劣质画家会成为官场和市场的宠儿，一个优秀画家反而长年坐冷板凳——当然啦，这种情况古今中外并不鲜见，只是我们时代过于突出。我在以前一篇文章里写道，那些宠儿是神笔马良，能画金山银山，就是进不了美术史。沈勤是个对自己要求很高的人，不将就，不随俗，或许“隐居”是不得已而为之，但毕竟给他一个安静思考和创作环境，对于他这样心性的人来说，自然是十分贴切的。几十年来，水墨领域花样百出，各色人等粉墨登场，现在看来，凑热闹易，出成果难——此情此景，沈勤便显得愈加难能可贵了。

!

从图式上看，沈勤的画是独一无二的——我想指出，独特的图式是一个画家是否优秀的主要指标。我参观过许多水墨画展览，不看作品展签，很难区分谁谁谁，千人一面是水墨画家的通病。我经常听到这些千人一面的画家自吹自擂：我是这个时代最好的画家。其实这些大言不惭的自我吹嘘者与两百年前的清朝人的绘画几乎雷同，而两百年的时间却从手指缝里溜走了。这是水墨画领域的奇葩现象：山头林立、人人“第一”、“大师”成堆，为什么所谓的国学、国术、国医之类行业中的骗子特别多？原因不外乎一点，他们忽悠那些吃瓜群众不需要多少本事。实际上，在当下的文化生态里，多元化、多样性的艺术类型、艺术表达已经大大挤压了水墨画的空间，如果弄水墨画的仍然躲在古人的隐蔽下原地踏步，举着“民族文化”的招牌，便以为可以放低标准，得到高分，结果只能是广场舞水准，图个热闹而已。我敢说，没有个人的独特图式，而且，这种图式必须与传统表达拉开极大的距离——或者在某种程度上，应该是全新的，唯一的，不可替代的，才有一点点可能与当代艺术试比高。我说一点点可能已经拔高了水墨画的意义。若干年时间里，我一直思考这个问题：水墨画有没有再创辉煌的前景？我的答案是悲观的。正如我和沈勤谈到，无论是现实还是未来，越是高端的水墨画将越是小众的，越缺乏大背景的支撑。因为真正能够创作出高品质水墨画的作者越来越少，真正能够欣赏这样高品质水墨画的人也越来越少。附带说一句，在很长时间内，许多头脑发热的人想把水墨画推向世界，使之变成“国际化语言”。我始终泼冷水：你们这么做，与孔子学院、太极拳、针灸、书法等等货色在国外下场一样，是在开“国际玩笑”。

!

回到沈勤作品的内部，看看他一个人悄悄练了“三十年”功，本事究竟如何？有一则笑话：某人躲在天山脚下练了一辈子功，从来不与人比试，只是号称自己天下无敌。笑话背后的意味当然一目了然，本事多大终究必须进入评价系统，否则就叫“笑话”。沈勤作画依旧运用传统媒材——前面说过，从中国画到水墨画，清理了概念的混杂，使其易于分辨，易于解说。就结果倒推起因，沈勤是个天生的水墨画家——我相信，他或许也可以做一些其它创作，但不会把他的细腻、敏感、空灵、通透如此完整地加以表达。在沈勤的水墨语言里，题材是不起多大作用的，对象即是他自身，听起来抽象，仔细辨析便可发现，他在作品里透射的自身的影子一直如画随行。至于技法，简直是他的一项绝活：层次的展开了无痕迹，薄如蝉翼，细如发丝；既灵动如水纹，又明晰如碧空，并恰到好处地与画面的大块黑白结构连接起来，水墨画的表现力因此凸显——清注意，这个表现力既与作者的才华和认识有关，也借力于媒材的特性。我特别想指出一点，文字语言在这里已经变得迟钝了，任何类比、隐喻都只能接近而难贴切。沈勤把水墨画的门槛从一厘米拔高到一米零一厘米，这是他的贡献，是他多年修为的结果。可以想象，一米多高的门槛，身边还会剩下多少跟随者？幸好，沈勤只关注自己的作品，以及作品能否在自己期待中更长久地延续下去。

一个画家的三十年，最终用作品证明了时间的胜利，这是我要说的全部意思。

                                                           2016·11·20

An Artist’s Thirty Years

Li Xiaoshan

For a long time Shen Qin has remained an unknown painter of Chinese ink and wash painting. In 1980s, his early works were well received, but later, in his own words, he lived in Shijiazhuang for 30 yearsas a recluse. It may be strange that Shen Qin chose to stay in Shijiazhuang instead of Naning where he original comes from. However, strange thing happens on a painter always, and it could not be ordinary more especially it happens on a great painter. In my eyes, Shen Qin is a great painter in the field of ink and wash painting and it’s hard to find someone to compete with him. You may doubt it because painting is not a boxing game that depends on power. But what I said “compete” is just a kind of metaphor. To some degree, a painter needs to work for many years, sometimes even up to 30 or 50 years to prove his or her painting skills or value. Usually, I would use the net and stones to explain the relation between time and painters - net stands for time and stones for painters: when a net is used to filter stones, the small-size stones will first fall out, then middle-size stones, and finally only the large-size stones can still stay in the net. It exemplifies that the one who can go through the sifting of the time can be really regarded as a great painter with reputation.

Chinese ink and wash painting at first was called Chinese painting. But since Chinese painting covers too many kinds of paintings which may cause people’s misunderstanding, it was finally renamed as ink and wash painting, categorized by the painting tools for making it easier to distinguish. In real life, as the traditional art, Chinese ink and wash painting falls into an awkward situation. Some of us love it and care about the development of it, but some of the simple-minded people and even the greedy people who dream of gaining without paying treat the Chinese ink and wash painting in a different. As Chinese ink and wash painting seems accessible for ordinaries and shares the natural affinity with people, the latter people can utilize it to earn their own profits easily. They are not different to some Chinese middle-aged women who can only dance in the simplest way in a large group yet surprisingly, audiences are willing to cheer and applaud them. Guys of the cheapest skills on painting are usually the most popular stars in various exhibitions. I used to joke with a director from a famous art academy: “in your academy, there are numbers of painters with the title of first-class or second-class who from day to day only repeattheir dull works and some of which even stay the level of stall painters, don’t you feel ashamed?” Here I have to explain myself that I do not mean to despise the stall painters because at least they work hard and earn their own livings. It is true to say that there are many followers painting ink and washing painting for its easy-handling. But there is no reason for them to create inferior-quality works that defile painting. The painters who work for the governmental academy are economically supported by the country and they should specially hold up their dignity to create first-class works for proving their value. The ancient China, including Tang, Song, Ming and Qing dynasties and even the first 20 years of the 20th century have witnessed generations of masters with abundant masterpieces. However, why the contemporary painters keep disappointing us? What they are supposed to dedicate is to catch up the ancestors and recover this painting.

Shen Qin does not talk too much, but that is enough for him to point out the essence of a topic. I get along with him well because we both feel sick of talking in an indirect way and cannot bear up with immoral things or behaviors. Once he talked to me that immoral painter is an apt characterization to immoral time. Indeed, he is right. But the question is why our time has joined in the trend of immorality? It is hard to answer this question because numerous interacting reasons can be involved in like mechanism, market, tradition and people’s mind etc. Though being complicated to answer, some core factors can be listed and in my view, the twisting standard is entailed. There are some proper standards for people to respect and follow. But when the standard is twisted, the world just becomes what William Shakespeare has described as turning black to white, fragrant to stink and beautiful to ugly. Once the standard is set aside, everything changes. In so doing, bad painters just cater to theofficialdom and market while the excellent painters are neglected by the public. This situation is common in any time and any place yet in our time it just goes beyond serious. Once I wrote in one of my articles that those inferior painters indeed could make themselves a millionaire, but they could never or ever enshrine their names in the history of paintings. In the contrary, Shen Qin is highly strict with himself and never flatter to the secular world. Frankly speaking, he did not really want to retire in Shijiazhuang, yet it did offer him a serene and peaceful environment to paint in conscience. In the past few decades, the style of Chinese ink and wash painting was changing rapidly and many painters emerged but achieved hardly. In this background, it is difficult to find any painter as pure as Shen Qin and that’s why he israre and commendable.

I'd like tohighlight that the schema is a fundamental standard to judge a painter and the schema of Shen Qin’s painting is very unique. After visiting many exhibitions of Chinese ink and wash painting, I find it hard to identify a painter without reading the caption. Painting with the same schema is a common problem for many paintersnowadays and they are ridiculous to tout themselves as No.1 painter in the world while actually the ancient painters in Qing dynasty have already created the artwork with the same schema initially. It’s a peculiar and odd situation in the field of Chinese ink and wash painting where numbers of so-called “masters” label themselves as the best. Why there are so many cheaters in the fields of sinology, Chinese painting and traditional Chinese medicine? The answer is very simple: the slightest effort is enough to cheat ordinary who are just laymen in these areas. The diversified and multidimensional art types and expressions nowadays have largely narrowed the space for the development of ink and wash painting. For these braggarts, raising the flag of carrying on and imitating the ancestors’ works are looked as the way to win themselves a noble reputation under a decreasing standard. However, they are totally wrong because their paintings are only as normal as square dance, where noise is the only focus. They lack their own schema of creating and the schema should maintain a distance from the ancient style or it should be totally fresh and irreplaceable to some extents, which is the only possible way for the ink and wash painting to compete with the modern arts. For many years, I have been keeping thinking of a question: can Chinese ink and wash painting regain its prosperity? My answer is negative. I have talked to Shen Qin that in the future, the more superb the ink and wash painting is, the more insufficient of its participants and support will be, as there are fewer painters that can create a printing of supreme quality and fewer appreciators either. By the way, many people have kept trying to raise a mania of ink and wash painting around the world. I always cast a cloud over this thought by saying if we really do so, we are just embarrassing ourselves globally as we have already failed in raising the international mania of Confucius Institute, Tai chi chuan, acupuncture and Chinese calligraphy.

Now let us go back and tap into his works to see whether his thirty-year practice could make any difference in his paintings. There is a joke that once there was a guy who kept practicing his skills but never tried to compete with anyone, and he still claimed himself as the top of the world. The meaning of this joke is obvious, that one’s ability should be evaluated so as to prove the ability is true; otherwise, the ability is just a joke. Shen Qin uses traditional materials to create art and undoubtedly he is a genius of Chinese ink and wash painting. I believe that he is able to draw other types of paintings, but Chinese ink and wash are still the best media for him to demonstrate his sentiment, sensitivity, purity and penetration. In his art language, the theme of painting does not play an important role as he expresses the reflection of himself in the paintings fully. It’s sounds quite abstract but if youanalyze his works, you will realize that his paintings can reflect himself. When it now comes to the painting skills, he is absolutely a master. He starts his painting in a sedate way and tips his brush softly on the art paper, as a butterfly skimming its wings on a lake or a bond of hair flying in the breeze. His brush glides as smoothly as a flowing river and leaves traces as clear as the blue sky. Moreover, perfect management of disposition of white spaces and black ones, as one can observe, is an indicator of his exploration into the interaction between water, ink and fabric of Chinese art paper. His talent and experience, with the help of the special materials as well, enable him to fully showcase his marvelous craft. One thing worthy of especially mentioning is that none of words, metaphor or analogy is suited enough to describe his great skills. Shen Qin has pushed Chinese ink and wash painting moving forward a step. This is his contribution and he deserves it after years of hard working and assiduous practice. After moving a step forward, there may still appear some followers behind him. Luckily, Shen Qin cares nothing but his works and hopes that his works can be passed down longer than he has thought.

If I were asked to conclude the whole article in on sentence, that is: during the thirty-year accumulation, a painter has finally proved the victory of time by his works.
