“倾斜”是一种必然的客观存在——倾斜总是对水平的偏移状态——存在总是难以稳定下来的相互关系。“偏斜的视界”也是一种必然的主观存在，是我们必然要占据的不同立场、要取的不同面向，这是一阵子的脆弱关系，是不断犹豫偏颇的眼界。

正如艺术家克劳德·克劳斯基所言，“朝向不同的视野，艺术家和公众所占据的位置立场通常是处在不断变换转移中的，《偏   见》就此提出和组织起一场对话。“

“偏   见”的无所不在和变幻莫测，它更加是我们已然领会的生存基础？是我们必然要取的生存策略和进路？展览和表演吸引观众和参与者，用自己的眼睛、脚步、手法，当然最重要的是自己的大脑和心思，亲自在场，体认我们不断转换的认知和行动问题。这蛊惑起一场集体生活中的相互揣测和关怀？这一知行的一念发动已然偏移，已然离开，同时也是向着他人而去？

《偏   见》展由获得2005年度马塞尔·杜尚奖（Prix Marcel Duchamp）的法国艺术家克劳德·克劳斯基和策展人王亚敏共同策划，法国艺术家艾尔莎·沃思、艺术团体得译工作室等参与展出和表演。

**克劳德·克劳斯基**

克劳德·克劳斯基获曾获法国“造型艺术大奖”，以及由ADIAF颁发的马塞尔·杜尚奖 (2005）。克劳德·克劳斯基绕开了正式的视觉艺术家训练。他于1982年进入ENSAD（国立高等装饰艺术学院）学习，但是第一年就退学，和Bla+Bla+Bla，Nina Childress，Jean Faucheur，Pierre Huyghe，Manhu， Ox，Trois carrés等创建了The Ripoulin Brothers街头艺术集体。1988年，他离开这一集体，使用自发朴素的、像是素描和印制这样的创作方式，开拓自己的独立工作。

他参加了里昂双年展（1995年），悉尼双年展（1996年），台北双年展（2000年）， 弗伦西亚双年展（2001年），沙迦双年展（2005年），威尼斯双年展（2017年）等。 2000年，Mudam（卢森堡艺术博物馆）委托他构建和组织其网站，为此他制作了一个专门用于互联网的杂志/画廊展示平台。该网站于2001年威尼斯双年展的卢森堡馆推出。它展示了Heath Bunting，FrançoisCurlet，Pierre Leguillon，Aleksandra Mir，Peter Kogler，David Shrigley等人的特制作品。自2005年以来，他在法国巴黎的国际高等美术学院任教。2012年，他策划了This & There展，以庆祝巴黎东京宫创造实验空间十周年，展览在74处空间展出74位艺术家的作品。

克劳斯基的创作主要是非物质的。语言是他的模型，用于呈现在我们的环境中收集的，或者在他的工作室中制作的图像，文本，数字和声音，虽然他并不愿去生产物体以及制造壮观的效果，但克劳斯基的工作仍然涉及视觉呈现和空间的挪用问题。克劳斯基的项目总是找到替代性的方式，从展示它们的网站所强加的形式中解放出来。他试图指出我们当代社会及其表征的矛盾，同时也质疑艺术作为文化共识和价值观的作用。他的作品面对并质疑我们所处的环境，艺术生产的条件和效益，以及它与观众的关系。

艾尔莎·沃思

艾尔莎·沃思生于1985年，2013年从巴黎国立高等美术学院获得硕士学位，目前生活和工作于巴黎。

艾尔莎对传统惯例比较看淡，对我们周遭的日常生机投以幽默的一瞥——一种同当代活动和仪式相联系的先行预制的姿态。运用多样化的媒介和噪杂的数据档案，艺术家想要运用相较于抵抗策略而言最微弱简单的方式和非景观化的姿态。艺术家质疑在日益方案化和管理化的环境下艺术作品的呈现以及再现系统。

艾尔莎的近期的个展包括：CAC l’Onde, Primo Piano, Galerie Martine Aboucaya Paris，巴黎，法国；Bazar Compatible Program, 上海，中国；Duplex/Walden, 日内瓦，瑞士等。艾尔莎近期参与的群展包括：MUDAM 卢森堡；Creux de l’Enfer；Centre d’art Bastille；Centre d’art Albert Chanot；ZK/U Center for Art等。目前艺术家正在全世界巡回其项目Potentiels évoqués visuels，这是一个旅行便携形式的展览，可以非常简单地布置在许多空间中。

得译工作室

得译工作室由夏意兰女士和保罗-德沃图（Paul DEVAUTOUR）先生2008年在上海一起创立。旨在立足上海，作为文化推手不定期组织各种文化讨论及艺术活动。探索当代艺术“特殊性”的反面：“兼容”的概念，以及今日艺术在非展示状态下进行的可能性。

夏意兰毕业于上海戏剧学院戏剧文学专业，并于巴黎文化艺术管理学院深造文化产业管理。曾先后就职于中法政府文化、旅游、教育等相关部门，现为上海得译文化艺术交流咨询有限公司负责人，致力于推动发展中法文化艺术交流工作。2011-2017年，得译工作室在上海一条小商品市场弄堂内开启了一个名为“兼容的盒子”的杂货铺实验空间，鼓励各路艺术家在此实践个人创意，共同完成项目152期。

Claude Closky

Closky won the "Grand Prix des Arts Plastiques" (1999) and the Marcel Duchamp Prize (2005)awarded by the ADIAF.Claude Closky has no formal training as a visual artist. He entered the ENSAD (Ecole National Supérieure des Arts Décoratifs) in 1982 but quit at the end of the first year to co-found The Ripoulin Brothers, a street artist collective, with Bla+Bla+Bla, Nina Childress, Jean Faucheur, Pierre Huyghe, Manhu, Ox, Trois carrés. In 1988 he left the group to develop his independent work, using voluntarily poor means such as drawing and printed matter.

He has participated in the Biennials of Lyon (1995), Sydney (1996), Taipei (2000), València (2001), Sharjah (2005), Venice (2017). In 2000, the Mudam (Luxembourg Art Museum) commissioned him to conceive and manage its website, for which he made a magazine and a gallery dedicated to the internet. The site was launched at the Luxembourg Pavilion in the 2001 Venice Biennale. It presented specific works by Heath Bunting, François Curlet, Pierre Leguillon, Aleksandra Mir, Peter Kogler, David Shrigley, among others. Since 2005 he has taught at the École Nationale Supérieure des Beaux-Arts in Paris. In 2012 he curated This & There, an exhibition to celebrate the tenth year of the Pavilion, Palais de Tokyo Laboratory for Creation (Paris) which presented the work of 74 artists in 74 different spaces.

The work of Claude Closky is mainly immaterial. Language is his model to articulate images, text, numbers, and sounds collected in our environment, or made in his studio. Although reluctant to producing objects and spectacular effects, Closky's work still addresses issues about visibility and space appropriation.Claude Closky's projects always find alternative ways to emancipate themselves from the formats imposed by the sites where they are exhibited. He seeks to point out the contradictions of our contemporary society and its representations, but also to question the role of art as producer of a cultural consensus and set of values. His works confront and question our environment, the conditions and benefits of artistic production, its relation to an audience.

Elsa Werth

Elsa Werth is an artist born in 1985. She currently works and lives in Paris. She holds a master’s degree in Fine Arts from École des Beaux-Arts de Paris (2013).

Elsa Werth makes light of conventions, and takes an amused look at ordinary animation that surrounds us in our daily lives —the prefabricated gestures connected to contemporary activity and rituals. Via a multiplicity of media and a clash of data and records, she claims to use minimal means and non-spectacular gestures which can be compared to resistance tactics. She calls into question the appearance of artworks and the systems of representation in a programmed and managed environment.

Her work has been presented in solo exhibitions in France (CAC l’Onde, Primo Piano, Galerie Martine Aboucaya Paris), in China (Bazar Compatible Program, Shanghai) and in Switzerland (Duplex/Walden, Geneva). Werth also participated in numerous group exhibitions (MUDAM Luxembourg, Creux de l’Enfer, Centre d’art Bastille, Centre d’art Albert Chanot, ZK/U Center for Art). She currently is circulating the project Potentiels évoqués visuels around the world, a traveling and portable exhibition format that can be set up in a number of spaces with a minimum of means.

DeYi Studio

DeYi Studio was founded in Shanghai in 2008 by XIA Yilan and Paul DEVAUTOUR. DeYi Studio is working on the concept of compatibility, as reversal of the concept of specificity, and explores the possibilities of a practice of unexposed art.

Xia Yilan studied scriptwriting in Shanghai Theater Academy, and cultural management in Paris. She leads Deyi Culture Consultants. Between 2011 and 2017 she opened a small space of experimentation in the alley of a bazaar in Shanghai and organize the "Bazaar Compatible Program" which hosts 152 projects of young artists.

About the exhibition

“Tilt Horizon” is an inevitable, objective existence. Tilting isthe unstable, shifting state of a given level, and existence is always an interactive relationship that is difficult to stabilize. However, “Tilt Horizon” is also an inevitable, subjective existence; it represents different positions that we must occupy, sub-stable relationships that face different directions, and visions that are constantly shifting and changing.

As the artist Claude Closky has said, “‘Tilt Horizon’proposes a dialogue about the always shifting places occupied by the artists and the public, confronting different horizons.”

“Tilt Horizon” is ubiquitous yet variable. More importantly, is it a foundation of existence that we fully understand? Is it a tactic or approach to existence that we must take? The exhibition and the performance attract viewers and participants, encouraging them to use their eyes, steps, gestures, and of course—and perhaps more importantly—their minds and thoughts. They are present to experience constantly changing and shifting thought and action. Does this induce collective speculation and concern for one another? Or has the idea and action already shifted, departing from yet moving towards someone else?

“Tilt Horizon” was co-curated by artist Claude Closky, winner of the 2005 Prix Marcel Duchamp; French artist Elsa Werth and art group DeYi Studio will participate in the exhibition and performance.
