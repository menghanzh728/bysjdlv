**策展:**

王亚敏   曲俊

“谁没有抱怨？谁能避免被抱怨？不是抱怨，而是普遍而深入骨髓的烦恼联结起我们。从传统威权到数字威权，从身体的直接侵犯到社交媒体的帖子大战，我们不是担心滚滚而来的下一个具体的侵犯和骂战，这些都会挺过去，但是莫名的烦恼却像一个幽灵一样萦绕占据着我们。是这种共同被困扰的感觉让我们心心相惜，一次次不断回到相互的抱怨、看不起和直接KO，就这样让我们成为不可分割的一群冤家。”

“在无尽的抱怨和共同的烦恼中，我们不断报之以灵机一动，飞快地拍摄，抖一抖语音，无尽的妙语连珠和吐槽；我们一直往底下流动生活，抖动着机灵。闲聊、好奇、仗义和妥协，争斗、拉拢和相互漠视，我们一边发着机灵的帖子和称赞，一边相互讨论、沟通和工作；是普遍的灵机一动让我们成为一群工作不分高低贵贱、劳动不分有无薪酬、职业不分临时固定、事业不分成功失败的一群。”

“这一切，都是当着大家的面，无论是被动还是主动的当面，到场还是监控的当面，是共同地当众灵机一动。这种共同的当众劳动，就是饱含了才华和民主的群众新路线，正是这种当众的批评、合作的闲聊锻造了新的群众，不仅仅是微信群的群众，也是街道、社区、城市、乡村、地区和国际的群众，也是恋人、家庭、团体和赛博格的群众。当然也是普遍的艺术工作者们的群众，是他们当众的口才让他们名正言顺地留在作为共和国的祖国，作为共和国的祖国就是当众的群众口才。从来不靠什么神仙皇帝，大家不是也日新月异了吗，新群众就是新民主。”

**Curator:**

Yamin Wang     Qu Jun

“Who has no complaints? Who can avoid being complained about? It’s not complaining; it’s a general worry that has seeped into our bones and bound us together. With the shift from traditional authoritarianism to digital authoritarianism and from the direct violation of bodies to battles waged on social media, we don’t worry about a specific fight or exchange of curses in the endless stream of posts. We can get over all of that, but an inexplicable worry haunts us like a ghost. It is this common feeling of being haunted that makes us cherish one another, coming back again and again to complain about, belittle, and “knock out” one  another. We have bonded together into an inseparable group of enemies.”

“With these endless complaints and collective worries, we have to constantly respond on impulse, sending quick snapshots and voice messages and coming up with never-ending complaints and quips. With these quick ideas, we are always living toward the bottom of society. Amidst this chatting, curiosity, loyalty, and compromise, as well as fighting, wooing, and mutual indifference, we are sending clever posts and likes on WeChat, but we are also talking, communicating, and working with one another. This general impulse makes us part of a group, whether our jobs are great or small, whether we are paid or not, whether our jobs are permanent or temporary, and whether our careers are failing or successful.”

“All of these impulses are public, infront of someone’s face—whether you are passively or actively in their presence, and whether you are alone or being monitored. This communal labor in public space—which contains both talent and democracy—is the new mass line. It is these public chats, both critical and collaborative, that produce new masses; these are not only the new masses of WeChat users, but also the new masses of streets, communities, villages, regions, and the world and the new masses of lovers, families, groups, and cyborgs. Of course, it is the public eloquence of the new masses of ordinary art workers in public space that gives them a place in a republic, a republic that is made up of the masses’ eloquence in public space. Even without the immortal emperor, we update and liberate ourselves every day—the new masses are the new democracy.”
