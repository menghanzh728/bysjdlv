比较好玩的是，林枞这批镜子绘画，当它们留在艺术家的工作室中的时候，画面本身自带了工作室特有的镜像。而当它们被放到当前特定展览空间中的时候，每一个经过和注目它们的人，以它们为背景的自拍等等，都被反射和吸纳，参与了这些镜子“绘画”自身新面貌的变化。

在神话中，纳西索斯爱上了自己在水里的倒影。神话是一种人造的、虚构的前历史。在现实中，在真正“人造”的历史中，人们造出了镜子来对镜自揽……直到今天，人们用人工智能手机自拍，并在人工制造的网络上分享。而这一人工网络并不是虚拟的神话，它是实实在在的，非常耗费人力、电力、矿藏和各种基础设施的全球电子讯息激荡，它反射和塑造了我们眼中的自身。

在某种古老的艺术史神话中，画家是在人造着世界的镜像；在某种更加神话化的现代主义绘画叙事中，画家是在人造着一种绘画物品：有一定厚度的色料平板；今天最吸引眼球的，譬如说像最近刚刚被接纳到MOMA展出的Refik Anadol的“讯息绘画”则是人造数据的人工智能处理物。对此，人们开始求教于人工智能的艺术批评来帮助自己欣赏它。

Lin Cong's batch of mirror paintings possesses a peculiar mirror-like quality when left in the artist's studio. However, when displayed in a specific exhibition space, every person who walks by and takes notice of them becomes reflected and absorbed, as they take selfies against the paintings. This interactive experience results in the transformation of the mirror "paintings" into new appearances.

In mythology, Narcissus famously fell in love with his own reflection in the water. While mythology is a man-made, fabricated pre-history, the creation of mirrors throughout human history is real. Today, people take selfies with AI smartphones and share them on an artificially created internet, which is a real, global electronic communication that consumes labor, energy, minerals, and various infrastructures. This technology plays a role in reflecting and shaping our self-image.

Throughout ancient art history, it was thought that painters create a mirror image of the world, while in a more modernist painting narrative, the painter creates a painting on a flat surface with a certain thickness of pigment. Contemporary artists like Refik Anadol push the boundaries of traditional mediums and exhibit their works, such as "Information Paintings," at MOMA. These artworks, which are artificially intelligent objects of data, require people to seek the help of AI art critics to appreciate them. https://hyperallergic.com/803577/we-asked-chatgpt-to-review-refik-anadols-unsupervised-at-moma/。

Lin Cong's mirror paintings combine mirrors and paintings, two seemingly traditional and enduring man-made media, but do they maintain their apparent "datedness" and still function as "chat-generated pre-training transformers" with the audience?

关于《现成品，林枞》

!

“破镜不重拾，落花难上枝”  \_《讯息即媒介》第一季 **第1集**现成品，林枞

"Shattered Mirrors Cannot be Reassembled, Fallen Flowers Cannot be Returned to the Branches"\_The Message is Medium  Episode 1

镜子和绘画，这些都是再传统和古老不过的自我观照的人工媒介。镜子加上绘画，这一丰富多样的传统组合让从古到今，让那些从传统到当代的艺术家们一直乐此不疲。

林枞说这一批镜子绘画作品仍旧是一种“现成品”的状态，我们可以理解为他所谓的“现成品”真的是现成的物品，而不是我们所熟悉的，艺术史上定义的“现成品艺术”。这批镜子绘画仍旧是徘徊在物品和作品之间，一方面是因为他有很多后续的继续创作的想法，让这批东西发展成为他的“电子遗迹”类型雕塑作品。一方面，我们觉得最为重要的，他的这批镜子绘画仍旧在当前和后续的展览现场持续不断地吸纳图像：每一个经过和注目它们的人，以它们为背景的自拍等等，都参与了这些现成品持续不断变化着的面貌的形成——在林枞最先提供给我们的作品图录中，它们仅仅带着它们在工作室中特有的反射图像。

林枞的这批镜子绘画主要是一批人工画在镜面上的古典雕塑的电子图像。这可能是一种最为古老和人工的讯息反射装置，没有任何智能可言，但又确实是一种不断中介着人和自身，和他熟悉的图像或者绘画形象之间的“聊天”，一套种生成预训练的旧智能系统，一种人肉chatGPT\_1.0？一种讯息雕塑？

从“聊天生成预训练转换器3.5”到“文心一言”，再到“生成预训练转换器4.0”，人以自身轨迹为内容，以各种关于人的可替代性版本为目的创造，似乎正在经历新一轮的迅速刷新。与此同时，如何看待那些仍旧不可替代的，似乎是人肉的，或者是传统的、旧的活动数据及其预训练模式呢？如何看待林枞目前看来是仍旧一种人工的“讯息雕塑"的这一批镜子现成品，以及期待它们未来的成品形态？一种真正的讯息雕塑应该是什么样的？

Mirrors and painting are both traditional and ancient mediums for self-reflection. This combination of tradition has kept artists from ancient to modern times constantly engaged.Lin Cong describes this batch of mirror-painting works as still in a "ready-made" state. We can understand that his "ready-made" refers to actual ready-made objects, rather than the "ready-made art" defined in art history. On the one hand, he has many ideas for creating ‘electronic relics’ type sculpture works based on this batch of pieces. On the other hand, what we find most important is that these ready-made mirror-paintings continue to absorb images at the exhibition site: every person passing by or gazing at them, taking selfies with them as backgrounds, etc., all participate in the constantly changing appearance of these ready-made pieces. In the artwork catalog provided by Lin Cong, they only carry their unique reflection images from his studio.Lin Cong's batch of mirror-painting works may be one of the oldest and most artificial information reflection devices, with no intelligence to speak of, but it is indeed a "chat" that continuously mediates between people and their familiar images or painting images (in this batch of mirror-paintings, mainly classical sculpture images or their electronic images), a set of old intelligent systems for generating pre-training, an information sculpture?
